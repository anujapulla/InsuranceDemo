Parametric vs. traditional insurance
Automatic trigger detection
Your policy pays the moment the threshold is crossed — no call needed, no claim filed. Third-party data from NOAA, USGS, or equivalent confirms the event.
24–48 hour payout
Funds arrive in your account within two business days of the trigger event — while you're still dealing with the immediate aftermath.
Zero paperwork
No receipts, no damage documentation, no inventory lists. The objective data trigger is the only evidence required.
Certainty of outcome
Payout amounts are fixed and pre-agreed. No depreciation, no adjuster discretion, no surprise deductions from your settlement.
Manual claims filing required
Policyholder must report damage, gather evidence, and submit a formal claim — often while still managing the immediate crisis.
45–90 day settlement timeline
Adjuster assignment, property inspection, damage assessment, negotiation, and final settlement can take months — sometimes longer after major events.
Adjuster discretion
Final payout depends on adjuster's assessment of actual damage — subject to depreciation calculations, coverage disputes, and interpretation of policy language.
Basis risk: none
Traditional insurance covers actual verified losses — a strength. Parametric may pay out even if your property wasn't damaged, or not pay if it was.
